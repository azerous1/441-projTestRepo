    If you're reading this, you're checking out the AirPod Pros. You want an honest opinion and insight from someone who has purchased them. You came to the right place. I bought these as a replacement for my Bose Truly Wireless earbuds. The features I was most interested in was the noise cancelation and the seamless integration with my Apple ecosystem. Apple did it again - easily one of the most feature-rich earbuds on the market. The noise cancelation is decent, the pairing is almost too easy, and with the software updates announced at WWDC 2020, you'll be hardpressed to find earbuds that are better suited for your iPhone, Mac or any other Apple device. So, why would I say they're amazing yet suck? They do everything but stay in my ear. The Bose earbuds have the "Stayhear" design, which, well, stays in your ear...no matter what! I've had the AirPod Pros fall out of my ear while just sitting there eating dinner. I haven't even tried running with them yet, let alone doing something more vigorous like playing basketball with them. With my Bose, I could play ball full go and not have them fall out. Being able to use earbuds when pushing myself when working out is the principle reason I buy wireless headphones. This is very important to me. There is nothing worse than having my earbuds pop out when I'm pushing myself. It's important to note that what I tried are the medium size buds. I did the sound test, and it came back as a "good" fit. I will be testing out the larger tips today...but I'm not hopeful. If the mediums pop out while eating ziti, I don't know that the larger ones will stand the stress test of a hard workout. If you don't intend to use them to exercise or you don't move around a lot, this might be an excellent choice for you. I'll keep you posted if you are interested in the feedback.

    Cant praise these AirPod Pros enough! Besides the awesome noice canceling, transparency, voice activation, accurate voice texting responses, reading texts aloud, call announcements, call clarity on both ends, 5 minutes of charge getting (41%) & 2 solid hours worth of constant music at 75% volume & 4 days of case battery power without recharging and small profile....there are only two things I am not a huge fan of. 1). Maybe its just the shape of my left ear but the AirPod Pros backing has a slight ridge. This ridge seems like it could be sanded down or should have been by Apple considering the price tag on these suckers. It doesnt bother my right ear but took a bit of getting used to on my left. It irritated my ear to where I had to find a comfortable position to be seated. I am not sure if Ive more or less gotten used to it now or if the burr has kind of worn down a bit. I checked a buddys Pros & his were the same way. Its worth mentioning because I know some people have complained in the past with EarPods not having a good universal fit. 2). It sounds odd but Im just being honest as some others may have or will experience this too. It seems like it creates moisture in the ear canal. I find myself wanting to take the AirPod Pro out am grabbing a Q-tip after wearing at times. Never experienced this while using my Wireless Powerbeats 3. Even after wearing them for literally hours on end. Battery on those things last ALL day. Once again, its awkward to even write this in my review but just want to give others an honest experience. On a side note, Im not a germaphobe but hygiene is high on my list. I stay clean so it isnt like Im not showering & popping them in my ears. Matter of fact, I may just use my Beats while working out since the AirPod Pros only come in white. My Pros will continue to be used outside of working out hours. Lastly, lack of a physical volume button is not an issue for me. If you have an Apple Watch, all your controls to include battery power are available right on your wrist. For those that do not, ask Siri to increase/decrease volume. You can even ask her to turn on/off noice cancellation or transparency modes and ask what your AirPods Pro battery level is. Overall, would like to give 5 stars but my two concerns kind of took that 5th one away.
